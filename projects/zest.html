Budget-Friendly Strongman Training

Affordable Indian Vegetarian Diet & Home Workout Plan

It's great that you're committed to your fitness goals and that you're looking for practical and budget-friendly ways to build muscle, especially with the aim of pursuing a Strongman championship. Here's a comprehensive plan that incorporates affordable Indian vegetarian foods, a muscle-gaining workout routine, and tips on how to maximize your resources.

Here are some Indian vegetarian foods that are rich in protein and can be included in your diet:

Paneer 18g High in protein; can be cooked in many ways.
Tofu 8g A versatile protein-rich food for vegetarians.
Eggs 6g (per egg) If you eat eggs, they're a great source of protein.
Greek Yogurt 10g Rich in protein and good for digestion.

Peanuts 25g High in protein and healthy fats.

Rajma (Kidney Beans) 8g Affordable and protein-rich.

Strength Training Routine

Since you don't have access to a gym, you can create your own home gym with basic materials like stones, bricks, or metal pipes. The focus is on compound movements, which are essential for building muscle mass and strength.

Weekly Routine:

Day 1: Push Day

  • Push-ups: 4 x 12-20 (Use a bench for incline push-ups)
  • Dips: 3 x 8-12 (Use sturdy chairs or parallel bars)
  • Overhead press: 4 x 10-12 (Use a heavy stone, bucket, or pipe)

Day 2: Pull Day

  • Pull-ups (or bodyweight rows): 4 x 8-10 (Use a strong horizontal bar or branches)
  • Bent-over rows: 4 x 10-12 (Use a heavy object like a stone)
  • Bicep curls: 3 x 10-12 (Use a stone or pipe)

Day 3: Leg Day

  • Squats: 4 x 15-20 (Bodyweight or with a stone for resistance)
  • Lunges: 4 x 12-15 per leg (Bodyweight or with stones)
  • Deadlifts: 4 x 8-12 (Use a stone or metal pipe)

Day 4: Core & Conditioning

  • Planks: 3 x 1-2 min (No equipment required)
  • Russian twists: 4 x 20 (Use a stone for weight)
  • Farmer's walks: 4 x 30 seconds (Carry stones or buckets)

Day 5: Active Rest

  • Light cardio: 20-30 minutes (walking, running, cycling)

Budget-Friendly Tips for Muscle Growth

Buy in Bulk

Purchase staples like rice, dal, and lentils in bulk to save money.

Make Your Own Protein Shake

If you want an alternative to whey protein, try mixing yogurt, milk, and peanut butter for a natural protein shake.

Use Leftovers

Save leftover dal, rice, and vegetables to use in your next meal.

Farmers Markets

Look for cheaper, fresh produce at local farmers' markets.

Grow Your Own Vegetables

If possible, growing some vegetables (like spinach or tomatoes) at home can save money.

Tracking Protein Intake

To calculate your protein needs, aim for 1.6–2.2g of protein per kg of body weight for muscle growth. Given your goal to reach 92 kg:

Protein intake goal = 92 kg * 2g = 184g of protein per day.

You can track your intake by:

  • Keeping a food diary or using a nutrition tracker app.
  • Measuring portion sizes (for example, a cup of dal = about 9g protein).
  • Checking nutritional labels on products like paneer and milk.

Cost-saving Alternatives for Supplements

Whey Protein Alternatives:

Make your own protein shakes with ingredients like milk, yogurt, eggs, and peanut butter.

Creatine Alternatives:

Creatine is found in meats, but you can support muscle growth with your regular diet rich in protein and carbs. Regular exercise and proper recovery will yield results over time.

Additional Advice

Consistency is Key

Stick to your diet and workout routine for steady muscle gain.

Get Enough Sleep

Muscle recovery happens during sleep, so aim for 7-9 hours.

Hydration

Drink plenty of water to support muscle function and recovery.

This plan should give you a solid foundation for building muscle and preparing for a Strongman challenge without breaking the bank. Stay consistent with your diet and workouts, and you'll see progress!
